Introduction:

The Employee Management System project is a comprehensive web application developed using Angular, designed to streamline HR and employee-related tasks within an organization. The system aims to provide a centralized platform for managing employee information, leave requests, and overall workforce administration. By leveraging Angular's capabilities, the application offers an intuitive and responsive user interface, making it accessible for both HR personnel and employees.


Authentication and Role-based Access:

The project incorporates a robust authentication system, distinguishing between HR and regular employees. Through the AuthService, the application verifies user credentials against stored data in the backend, allowing secure access to specific features based on roles. HR members enjoy access to a dedicated dashboard with functionalities tailored to their administrative responsibilities, while employees have access to features relevant to their needs.


Employee Data Management:

The heart of the system lies in efficient employee data management facilitated by the EmployeeService. This service enables CRUD operations on employee records, ensuring that HR can add, edit, and delete employee information as needed. The service also plays a crucial role in notifying components to refresh the employee list in real-time, providing an up-to-date view of the workforce.


Leave Management:

The Employee Management System streamlines the leave application process through the LeaveService. Employees can submit leave requests, view their leave history, and check their leave balances. For HR personnel, the system integrates a dedicated leave management component (HrLeaveComponent) within the HR dashboard. This component displays pending leave requests and provides HR with the ability to approve or reject them.


Online Status:

The system may track whether an employee is currently online or offline. This status is useful for communication tools, allowing employees to know who is available for real-time interactions.


Leave Status:

The system should maintain information about an employee's leave status, indicating whether they are on leave, the type of leave and the duration of the leave
